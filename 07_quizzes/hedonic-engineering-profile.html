The Scoreboard

The only difference between an alchemist and an addict is the scoreboard.

Both use the same levers. Both seek state change. But the alchemist tracks what works, notices what costs too much, and builds a repeatable practice. The addict chases the next hit without integration.

Your matrix is not a menu of techniques. It is a map of how you change state. Use it to ask:

  • Where am I already well regulated?
  • Where do I habitually overdo or avoid?
  • Which levers could I engage more gently?
  • Which ones deserve more respect?

Small, repeatable shifts reshape baseline more reliably than rare extremes.

You do not need to become someone else. You only need to stack what already works for you — and track the results.
